\section{HP filter}

HP filter: decompose economic series into trend and cyclical component
\[
y_t = y_t^c + y_t^T
\]
\[
\min_{\left\{y_t^T\right\}_{t=1}^N}
\mathcal L
=
\underbrace{\sum_{t=1}^N \left(y_t-y_t^T\right)^2}_{\textstyle \left(1\right)}
+
\lambda\,
\underbrace{\sum_{t=2}^{N-1}
\left[
\left(y_{t+1}^T-y_t^T\right)-\left(y_t^T-y_{t-1}^T\right)
\right]^2}_{\textstyle \left(2\right)}
\]

Notes:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textcolor{red}{\arabic*)}]
\item penalty of calling everything trend
\item change in trend growth rate, penalty of wiggles
\end{enumerate}

\textcolor{red}{If $\lambda$ huge, close to straight line in logs.}

\textcolor{red}{If $\lambda=0$, wiggle a lot, fit to $y_t$.}

Define
\[
\Delta^2 y_t^T
\equiv
y_{t+1}^T - 2y_t^T + y_{t-1}^T
\qquad
\text{(second-order difference)}
\]

\[
\frac{\partial \mathcal L}{\partial y_t^T}=0
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
\frac{\partial \left(1\right)}{\partial y_t^T}
=
2\left(y_t^T-y_t\right)
\]

Since
\[
\Delta^2 y_{t-1}^T = y_t^T -2y_{t-1}^T + y_{t-2}^T
\qquad \text{(coefficient $+1$)}
\]
\[
\Delta^2 y_t^T = y_{t+1}^T -2y_t^T + y_{t-1}^T
\qquad \text{(coefficient $-2$)}
\]
\[
\Delta^2 y_{t+1}^T = y_{t+2}^T -2y_{t+1}^T + y_t^T
\qquad \text{(coefficient $+1$)}
\]

\[
\frac{\partial}{\partial y_t^T}
\sum_{s=2}^{N-1} \left(\Delta^2 y_s^T\right)^2
=
2\Delta^2 y_{t-1}^T \frac{\partial \Delta^2 y_{t-1}^T}{\partial y_t^T}
+
2\Delta^2 y_t^T \frac{\partial \Delta^2 y_t^T}{\partial y_t^T}
+
2\Delta^2 y_{t+1}^T \frac{\partial \Delta^2 y_{t+1}^T}{\partial y_t^T}
\]
\[
=
2\left(
\Delta^2 y_{t-1}^T - 2\Delta^2 y_t^T + \Delta^2 y_{t+1}^T
\right)
\]

Since
\[
\Delta^2 y_{t-1}^T = y_t^T - 2y_{t-1}^T + y_{t-2}^T,
\]
\[
-2\Delta^2 y_t^T
=
-2\left(y_{t+1}^T - 2y_t^T + y_{t-1}^T\right)
=
-2y_{t+1}^T + 4y_t^T - 2y_{t-1}^T,
\]
\[
\Delta^2 y_{t+1}^T
=
y_{t+2}^T -2y_{t+1}^T + y_t^T,
\]
we get
\[
\frac{\partial \mathcal L}{\partial y_t^T}
=
\frac{\partial \left(1\right)}{\partial y_t^T}
+
\lambda
\frac{\partial}{\partial y_t^T}
\sum_{s=2}^{N-1}\left(\Delta^2 y_s^T\right)^2
=0.
\]
\[
\Rightarrow\quad
\left(1+6\lambda\right)y_t^T
-4\lambda y_{t-1}^T
-4\lambda y_{t+1}^T
+\lambda y_{t-2}^T
+\lambda y_{t+2}^T
=
y_t,
\qquad
3\le t\le N-2.
\]

At $t=1$ (only $\Delta^2 y_2^T$ exists),
\[
\left(1+\lambda\right)y_1^T - 2\lambda y_2^T + \lambda y_3^T = y_1.
\]

At $t=2$ (only $\Delta^2 y_2^T$ and $\Delta^2 y_3^T$ exist),
\[
-2\lambda y_1^T + \left(1+5\lambda\right)y_2^T -4\lambda y_3^T + \lambda y_4^T = y_2.
\]

Symmetrically for $t=N$ and $t=N-1$, collect $\left\{y_t\right\}$ into the $N\times 1$ vector $y$ and $\left\{y_t^T\right\}$ into $y^T$, then we have
\[
A y^T = y
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
y^T = A^{-1} y
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
y^c = y - A^{-1} y.
\]

where
\[
A=
\begin{pmatrix}
1+\lambda & -2\lambda & \lambda & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & 0\\
-2\lambda & 1+5\lambda & -4\lambda & \lambda & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & 0\\
\lambda & -4\lambda & 1+6\lambda & -4\lambda & \lambda & \cdots & 0 & 0 & 0\\
0 & \lambda & -4\lambda & 1+6\lambda & -4\lambda & \ddots & 0 & 0 & 0\\
\vdots & & \ddots & \ddots & \ddots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots\\
0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & \lambda & -4\lambda & 1+6\lambda & -4\lambda & \lambda\\
0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & \lambda & -4\lambda & 1+5\lambda & -2\lambda\\
0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & \lambda & -2\lambda & 1+\lambda
\end{pmatrix}.
\]

Let $D$ be the $\left(N-2\right)\times N$ second-difference matrix, whose $t$-th row ($t=2,\dots,N-1$) looks like
\[
\left[\,0\ \cdots\ 1\ -2\ 1\ \cdots\ 0\,\right]
\]
so that
\[
\left(Dy^T\right)_{t-1}=\Delta^2 y_t^T.
\]

The HP objective becomes
\[
\min_{y^T}\ \left(y-y^T\right)'\left(y-y^T\right)+\lambda \left(Dy^T\right)'\left(Dy^T\right).
\]

F.O.C.
\[
-2\left(y-y^T\right)+2\lambda D'Dy^T=0
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
\left(I+\lambda D'D\right)y^T=y.
\]

Hence
\[
A=I+\lambda D'D.
\]

As a 5-diagonal pattern:
\[
\text{interior diagonal: } \left[\,0\ \cdots\ 1\ -2\ 1\ \cdots\ 0\,\right]
\begin{bmatrix}
0\\
\vdots\\
1\\
-2\\
1\\
\vdots\\
0
\end{bmatrix}
=6
\]

\[
\text{first-off diagonal: } \left[\,0\ \cdots\ 1\ -2\ 1\ 0\ \cdots\ 0\,\right]
\begin{bmatrix}
0\\
\vdots\\
0\\
1\\
-2\\
1\\
0\\
\vdots\\
0
\end{bmatrix}
=-4
\]

\[
\text{second-off diagonal: } \left[\,0\ \cdots\ 1\ -2\ 1\ 0\ 0\ \cdots\ 0\,\right]
\begin{bmatrix}
0\\
\vdots\\
0\\
0\\
1\\
-2\\
1\\
0\\
\vdots\\
0
\end{bmatrix}
=1
\]

\[
\lambda\uparrow
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
\text{the smoother the series } y_t^T.
\]

\paragraph{Pros}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\arabic*)]
\item Simple and transparent. (defined by one objective)
\item Produces a smooth trend with tunable smoothness $\lambda$
\item Widely used in Macro
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Cons}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\arabic*)]
\item End-point problem: start and end of sample is distorted.
\item $\lambda$ sensitivity: moments (volatility, correlations) change a lot with $\lambda$.
\item Spurious dynamics: HP can induce artificial serial correlations and distort cross-corr between series (create cycles).
\item Leakage: low-frequency cycles leak into trend.
\item Not structural: no model interpretation.
\end{enumerate}

Why $\lambda=1600$ is the quarterly standard?

Rule of thumb:
\[
\lambda = 100\times \left(\text{periods per year}\right)^2.
\]
Hence
\[
\lambda_{\text{quarter}} = 100\times 4^2 = 1600,
\qquad
\lambda_{\text{month}} = 100\times 12^2 = 14400,
\qquad
\lambda_{\text{annual}} = 100\times 1^2 = 100.
\]

\section{Long and Plosser (1983, JPE)}

Social planner's Problem Setup:
\[
U=\max_{\left\{C_t,L_t,K_t\right\}}
E_0\left\{
\sum_{t=0}^{\infty}\beta^t
\left[
\theta \ln C_t + \left(1-\theta\right)\ln L_t
\right]
\right\}
\]

s.t.
\[
K_t = Z_t K_{t-1}^{\alpha} N_t^{1-\alpha} - C_t
\qquad \text{and } K_0 \text{ is given.}
\]
Resource constraint:
\[
C_t + K_t = Y_t.
\]

\[
N_t + L_t = 1
\qquad \text{(trade-off)}
\]

\[
\textcolor{red}{L:\ \text{leisure} \qquad N:\ \text{working hour}.}
\]

\[
\textcolor{red}{\text{Total time is normalized to }1.}
\]

where
\[
\log Z_t = \left(1-\psi\right)\log \bar Z + \psi \log Z_{t-1} + \varepsilon_t,
\qquad
\varepsilon_t \overset{i.i.d.}{\sim} N\left(0,\sigma^2\right),
\qquad
\psi<1.
\]

Note: capital completely turns over,
\[
\left(1-\delta\right)K_{t-1}=0
\qquad \delta=1.
\]

Lagrangian:
\[
\mathcal L
=
E_0\sum_{t=0}^{\infty}\beta^t
\left[
\theta \ln C_t + \left(1-\theta\right)\ln\left(1-N_t\right)
+
\lambda_t\left(Z_t K_{t-1}^{\alpha}N_t^{1-\alpha}-C_t-K_t\right)
\right].
\]

\[
\frac{\partial \mathcal L}{\partial C_t}=0
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
\frac{\theta}{C_t}=\lambda_t
\tag{1}
\]
\[
\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial N_t}=0
\qquad \Rightarrow \qquad
-\underbrace{\frac{1-\theta}{1-N_t}}_{\text{utility loss}}
+\underbrace{\lambda_t}_{\text{MUC}_t}
\underbrace{\left(1-\alpha\right)Z_tK_{t-1}^{\alpha}N_t^{-\alpha}}_{\text{MPN}_t}
=0
\]
\[
\Rightarrow\quad
\frac{1-\theta}{1-N_t}
=
\left(1-\alpha\right)\lambda_t Z_t K_{t-1}^{\alpha} N_t^{-\alpha}
\tag{2}
\]
\textcolor{red}{\qquad \text{labor-leisure condition (intratemporal)}}

\[
\frac{\partial \mathcal L}{\partial K_t}=0
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
-\lambda_t \beta^t
+
\beta^{t+1}E_t
\left[
\lambda_{t+1}
\frac{\partial}{\partial K_t}
\left(
Z_{t+1}K_t^{\alpha}N_{t+1}^{1-\alpha}
\right)
\right]
=0
\]
\[
\Rightarrow\quad
\lambda_t
=
\beta E_t\left(
\lambda_{t+1}\alpha Z_{t+1}K_t^{\alpha-1}N_{t+1}^{1-\alpha}
\right)
\tag{3}
\]
\textcolor{red}{\qquad \text{Euler equation (intertemporal)}}

TVC:
\[
\lim_{T\to\infty}\beta^T U_C\left(C,L\right)K_{T+1}=0.
\tag{4}
\]

\paragraph{Closed-form solution: constant savings rate and constant labor}
\mbox{}\\
Tractability: log utility + Cobb--Douglas + full depreciation
\[
\Rightarrow\quad \text{constant savings rate / hours.}
\]

Since
\[
Y_{t+1}=Z_{t+1}K_t^\alpha N_{t+1}^{1-\alpha},
\]
we have
\[
\alpha Z_{t+1}K_t^{\alpha-1}N_{t+1}^{1-\alpha}
=
\alpha\frac{Y_{t+1}}{K_t}.
\]

Plug into (3):
\[
\lambda_t = \beta E_t\left(\lambda_{t+1}\alpha\frac{Y_{t+1}}{K_t}\right).
\]

Using (1):
\[
\frac{\theta}{C_t}
=
\beta E_t\left(
\frac{\theta}{C_{t+1}}\alpha\frac{Y_{t+1}}{K_t}
\right)
\]
\[
\Rightarrow\quad
\frac{1}{C_t}
=
\beta\alpha E_t\left[
\frac{Y_{t+1}}{C_{t+1}}\frac{1}{K_t}
\right].
\tag{5}
\]

Guess and verify:
\[
C_t=\left(1-s\right)Y_t,
\qquad
s \text{ is the savings rate},
\qquad
K_t=sY_t.
\qquad \text{(full depreciate)}
\]

Then
\[
\frac{Y_{t+1}}{C_{t+1}}=\frac{1}{1-s}.
\]
Plug into (5):
\[
\frac{1}{\left(1-s\right)Y_t}
=
\alpha\beta
\frac{1}{1-s}
\frac{1}{sY_t}
\]
\[
\Rightarrow\quad
s=\alpha\beta.
\]

Hence
\[
C_t=\left(1-\alpha\beta\right)Y_t,
\qquad
K_t=\alpha\beta\,Y_t.
\]
\[
\left\{
\begin{aligned}
&\alpha\uparrow
\;\Rightarrow\;
\text{capital income share}\uparrow
\;\Rightarrow\;
\text{savings rate}\uparrow,\\[0.4em]
&\beta\uparrow
\;\Rightarrow\;
\text{discount factor}\downarrow
\;\Rightarrow\;
\text{savings rate}\uparrow.
\end{aligned}
\right.
\]
Plug into (2):
\[
\frac{1-\theta}{1-N_t}
=
\left(1-\alpha\right)\frac{\theta}{C_t}\frac{Y_t}{N_t}
=
\left(1-\alpha\right)\frac{\theta}{\left(1-\alpha\beta\right)Y_t}\frac{Y_t}{N_t}
\]
\[
\Rightarrow\quad
\left(1-\theta\right)\left(1-\alpha\beta\right)N_t
=
\left(1-\alpha\right)\theta\left(1-N_t\right)
\]
\[
\Rightarrow\quad
N_t=\bar N
=
\frac{\theta\left(1-\alpha\right)}
{\theta\left(1-\alpha\right)+\left(1-\theta\right)\left(1-\alpha\beta\right)}.
\]

\paragraph{Labor market equilibrium}
\mbox{}\\
Household's problem:
\[
U_{N,t}+\lambda_t W_t=0
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
U_{N,t}+U_{C,t}W_t=0
\]
\textcolor{red}{\qquad \text{labor supply}}

Firm's problem:
\[
\max_{K_{t-1},N_t}\ \Pi_t = Y_t - W_tN_t - R_tK_{t-1}
\]
\textcolor{red}{\qquad \text{labor demand}}

Hence
\[
W_t
= Z_t \frac{Y_t}{N_t}\left(1-\alpha\right).
\]

From household labor supply:
\[
W_t=\frac{1-\theta}{\theta}\frac{C_t}{1-N_t}.
\]

Equivalently,
\[
W_t
=
\frac{1-\theta}{\theta}
\frac{\left(1-\alpha\beta\right) Z_t K_{t-1}^{\alpha}N_t^{1-\alpha}}
{1-N_t}.
\]
From firm labor demand:
\[
W_t=\left(1-\alpha\right)Z_tK_{t-1}^{\alpha}N_t^{-\alpha}.
\]


A positive $Z_t$ or $K_{t-1}$ shock shifts labor supply up by the same factor as labor demand:
\[
Z_t K_{t-1}^{\alpha}.
\]

Hence labor supply's wealth effect is exactly offset by labor demand increase.
So when \(Z_t\) rises, the real wage increases. This generates a substitution effect toward more labor, but also an income effect toward more leisure.

\[
W_t=\left(1-\alpha\right)Z_tK_{t-1}^{\alpha}N_t^{-\alpha}
\]

\[
Z_t\uparrow
\;\Rightarrow\;
MPN_t\uparrow
\;\Rightarrow\;
W_t\uparrow
\;\Rightarrow\;
\text{labor demand curve shifts up}
\]

\[
Z_t\uparrow
\;\Rightarrow\;
Y_t\uparrow
\;\Rightarrow\;
C_t=\left(1-\alpha\beta\right)Y_t\uparrow
\;\Rightarrow\;
MU_{C,t}\downarrow
\;\Rightarrow\;
N_t\downarrow
\qquad
\text{Wealth effect}
\]

\[
\begin{aligned}
\underbrace{\frac{1-\theta}{1-N_t}}_{\text{marginal disutility of working}}
&=
\underbrace{\lambda_tW_t}_{\text{marginal utility value of wage income}}
=
\underbrace{MU_{C,t}W_t}_{\text{intra-temporal optimality}}
=
\frac{\theta}{C_t}\left(1-\alpha\right)\frac{Y_t}{N_t}
\\[8pt]
&=
\frac{\theta\left(1-\alpha\right)}{N_t}\left(\frac{Y_t}{C_t}\right)
=
\frac{\theta\left(1-\alpha\right)}{1-\alpha\beta}\frac{1}{N_t}.
\end{aligned}
\]

\section{Steady state}

At steady state:
\[
K_t=K_{t-1}=\bar K,
\qquad
C_t=C_{t+1}=\bar C,
\qquad
N_t=N_{t+1}=\bar N.
\]

\[
\bar K = \bar Z \bar K^\alpha \bar N^{1-\alpha} + \left(1-\delta\right)\bar K - \bar C
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
\bar C=\bar Z\bar K^\alpha \bar N^{1-\alpha}-\delta\bar K.
\tag{1}
\]

\[
1
=
\alpha\beta \bar Z \bar K^{\alpha-1}\bar N^{1-\alpha}
+
\left(1-\delta\right)\beta.
\tag{2}
\]

\[
A\bar C=\left(1-\bar N\right)\left(1-\alpha\right)\bar Z\bar K^\alpha \bar N^{-\alpha}.
\tag{3}
\]

\[
\Rightarrow\quad
\bar Z\,\bar K^\alpha \bar N^{1-\alpha}-\delta \bar K
=
\frac{1}{A}\left(\left(1-\alpha\right)\bar Z\,\bar K^\alpha \bar N^{-\alpha}
- \left(1-\alpha\right)\bar Z\,\bar K^\alpha \bar N^{1-\alpha}\right)
\]

\[
\Rightarrow\quad
\bar Z\,\bar K^{\alpha-1}\bar N^{1-\alpha}
+
\frac{1-\alpha}{A}\,\bar Z\,\bar K^{\alpha-1}\bar N^{1-\alpha}
=
\frac{1-\alpha}{A}\,\bar Z\,\bar K^{\alpha-1}\bar N^{-\alpha}
+\delta
\]

\[
\Rightarrow\quad
\bar Z\,\bar K^{\alpha-1}\bar N^{1-\alpha}
=
\frac{A}{1-\alpha+A}
\left[
\frac{1-\alpha}{A}\,\bar Z\,\bar K^{\alpha-1}\bar N^{-\alpha}
+\delta
\right]
\]

Using (2), after simplification one gets
\[
\bar N
=
\left\{
1+\frac{A}{1-\alpha}
\left[
1-\frac{\alpha\beta\delta}{1-\beta\left(1-\delta\right)}
\right]
\right\}^{-1}.
\]

Then
\[
\bar K
=
\left[
\frac{1-\beta\left(1-\delta\right)}{\bar Z\alpha\beta}
\bar N^{\alpha-1}
\right]^{\frac{1}{\alpha-1}}
=
\bar N
\left[
\frac{\bar Z\alpha}{\beta^{-1}-\left(1-\delta\right)}
\right]^{\frac{1}{1-\alpha}}.
\]

And
\[
\bar C
=
\bar Z\,\bar K^\alpha \bar N^{\,1-\alpha}
-\delta \bar K
=
\bar N^{\alpha} \cdot
\left[
\frac{\bar Z \alpha}{\beta^{-1}-\left(1-\delta\right)}
\right]^{\frac{\alpha}{1-\alpha}}
\bar N^{1-\alpha}\bar Z
-\delta \bar K
\]

\[
=
\bar Z^{\frac{1}{1-\alpha}}
\bar N
\left[
\frac{\alpha}{\beta^{-1}-\left(1-\delta\right)}
\right]^{\frac{\alpha}{1-\alpha}}
-\delta \bar N\,\bar Z^{\frac{1}{1-\alpha}}
\left[
\frac{\alpha}{\beta^{-1}-\left(1-\delta\right)}
\right]^{\frac{1}{1-\alpha}}
\]

\[
=
\bar N\,\bar Z^{\frac{1}{1-\alpha}}
\left[
\left(
\frac{\alpha}{\beta^{-1}-\left(1-\delta\right)}
\right)^{\frac{\alpha}{1-\alpha}}
-\delta
\left(
\frac{\alpha}{\beta^{-1}-\left(1-\delta\right)}
\right)^{\frac{1}{1-\alpha}}
\right]
\]
\paragraph{Derive the log-linearized system}\mbox{}\\
(i) TFP process:
\[
\log Z_t=\left(1-\psi\right)\log \bar Z+\psi \log Z_{t-1}+\varepsilon_t
\]
Let
\[
Z_t=\bar Z e^{\hat z_t}.
\]
Then
\[
\hat z_t + \log \bar Z
=
\left(1-\psi\right)\log \bar Z + \psi \log \bar Z + \psi \hat z_{t-1} + \varepsilon_t
\]
\[
\Rightarrow\quad
\hat z_t=\psi \hat z_{t-1}+\varepsilon_t.
\]

(ii)
\[
K_t=Z_tK_{t-1}^{\alpha}N_t^{1-\alpha}+\left(1-\delta\right)K_{t-1}-C_t.
\]

Substitute
\[
K_t=\bar K\left(1+\hat k_t\right),
\quad
Z_t=\bar Z\left(1+\hat z_t\right),
\quad
K_{t-1}=\bar K\left(1+\hat k_{t-1}\right),
\quad
N_t=\bar N\left(1+\hat n_t\right),
\quad
C_t=\bar C\left(1+\hat c_t\right),
\]
and keep only first-order terms:
\[
\bar K+\bar K\hat k_t
=
\bar Z\bar K^\alpha \bar N^{1-\alpha}
\left(1+\hat z_t\right)\left(1+\alpha \hat k_{t-1}\right)\left(1+\left(1-\alpha\right)\hat n_t\right)
+\left(1-\delta\right)\bar K+\left(1-\delta\right)\bar K\hat k_{t-1}
-\bar C-\bar C\hat c_t.
\]

Using the steady state condition and dropping higher-order terms:
\[
\bar K\hat k_t
=
\alpha \bar Y \hat k_{t-1}
+
\bar Y \hat z_t
+
\left(1-\alpha\right)\bar Y \hat n_t
+
\left(1-\delta\right)\bar K \hat k_{t-1}
-
\bar C \hat c_t,
\]
where
\[
\bar Y\equiv \bar Z\bar K^\alpha \bar N^{1-\alpha}.
\]

Hence
\[
\hat k_t
=
\left(\alpha\frac{\bar Y}{\bar K}+1-\delta\right)\hat k_{t-1}
+
\left(1-\alpha\right)\frac{\bar Y}{\bar K}\hat n_t
+
\frac{\bar Y}{\bar K}\hat z_t
-
\frac{\bar C}{\bar K}\hat c_t.
\]

(iii)
\[
AC_t=\left(1-N_t\right)\left(1-\alpha\right)Z_tK_{t-1}^{\alpha}N_t^{-\alpha}.
\]

Log-linearizing gives
\[
\hat n_t
=
\left(\alpha+\frac{\bar N}{1-\bar N}\right)^{-1}
\left(
\hat z_t+\alpha \hat k_{t-1}-\hat c_t
\right).
\]

(iv) Euler equation:
\[
1=\beta E_t\left[
\frac{C_t}{C_{t+1}}
\left(
\alpha Z_{t+1}K_t^{\alpha-1}N_{t+1}^{1-\alpha}+1-\delta
\right)
\right].
\]

Let
\[
M_{t+1}\equiv \alpha Z_{t+1}K_t^{\alpha-1}N_{t+1}^{1-\alpha},
\qquad
G_{t+1}\equiv M_{t+1}+1-\delta.
\]

Then
\[
\hat m_{t+1}
=
\hat z_{t+1}+\left(\alpha-1\right)\hat k_t+\left(1-\alpha\right)\hat n_{t+1}.
\]

Define
\[
\tilde\delta\equiv \frac{\bar M}{\bar G}.
\]
Since
\[
\bar G=\beta^{-1},
\qquad
\bar G=\bar M+\left(1-\delta\right),
\]
we have
\[
\tilde\delta
=
\frac{\bar M}{\bar G}
=
1-\frac{1-\delta}{\bar G}
=
1-\beta\left(1-\delta\right).
\]

Thus
\[
G_{t+1}\approx \bar G\left(1+\tilde\delta \hat m_{t+1}\right).
\]

Also
\[
\frac{C_t}{C_{t+1}}
=
\frac{\bar C e^{\hat c_t}}{\bar C e^{\hat c_{t+1}}}
=
e^{\hat c_t-\hat c_{t+1}}
\approx
1+\hat c_t-\hat c_{t+1}.
\]

So Euler equation becomes
\[
1
=
\beta E_t\left[
\bar G \left(1+\hat c_t-\hat c_{t+1}+\tilde\delta \hat m_{t+1}\right)
\right]
\]
Since
\[
\beta \bar G=1,
\]
we obtain
\[
E_t\left[
\hat c_t-\hat c_{t+1}+\tilde\delta \hat m_{t+1}
\right]=0.
\]

Hence
\[
\hat c_t
=
E_t\hat c_{t+1}
-
\tilde\delta E_t\left[
\hat z_{t+1}-\left(1-\alpha\right)\hat k_t+\left(1-\alpha\right)\hat n_{t+1}
\right].
\]

If you have CRRA,
\[
\frac{C_t}{C_{t+1}} \to \left(\frac{C_t}{C_{t+1}}\right)^\sigma
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
\sigma \hat c_t
=
\sigma E_t\hat c_{t+1}
-
E_t\hat r_{t+1}.
\]

Thus we have a system of equations:
\[
\begin{cases}
\hat z_t=\psi \hat z_{t-1}+\varepsilon_t,\\[0.5em]
\hat c_t
=
E_t\hat c_{t+1}
-
\tilde\delta E_t\left[
\hat z_{t+1}-\left(1-\alpha\right)\hat k_t+\left(1-\alpha\right)\hat n_{t+1}
\right],\\[0.5em]
\hat n_t
=
\left(\alpha+\dfrac{\bar N}{1-\bar N}\right)^{-1}
\left(\hat z_t+\alpha\hat k_{t-1}-\hat c_t\right),\\[1em]
\hat k_t
=
\left(\alpha\frac{\bar Y}{\bar K}+1-\delta\right)\hat k_{t-1}
+
\left(1-\alpha\right)\frac{\bar Y}{\bar K}\hat n_t
+
\frac{\bar Y}{\bar K}\hat z_t
-
\frac{\bar C}{\bar K}\hat c_t.
\end{cases}
\]

Four equations, four variables:
\[
\left(\hat k_{t-1},\hat c_t,\hat n_t,\hat z_t\right).
\]

\[
\hat k_{t-1}\text{ is predetermined variable,}
\qquad
\hat z_t \text{ follows a stable AR(1) process,}
\]
\[
\left(\hat c_t,\hat n_t\right) \text{ are jump variables.}
\]

Use undetermined coefficient method; guess
\[
\hat k_t = V_{kk}\hat k_{t-1}+V_{kz}\hat z_t,
\qquad
\hat c_t = V_{ck}\hat k_{t-1}+V_{cz}\hat z_t,
\qquad
\hat n_t = V_{nk}\hat k_{t-1}+V_{nz}\hat z_t.
\]

Then
\[
\hat k_t
=
V_{kz}
\sum_{i=0}^{\infty}\sum_{j=0}^{i}
V_{kk}^{\,j}\psi^{\,i-j}\varepsilon_{t-i},
\qquad
\hat z_t=\sum_{s=0}^{\infty}\psi^s\varepsilon_{t-s},
\]
and since
\[
\hat y_t=\alpha\hat k_{t-1}+\left(1-\alpha\right)\hat n_t+\hat z_t,
\]
we can write
\[
\hat y_t
=
V_{yz}\varepsilon_t
+
\sum_{i=0}^{\infty}
\left(
V_{yk}V_{kz}\sum_{j=0}^{i}V_{kk}^{\,j}\psi^{\,i-j}
+V_{yz}\psi^{i+1}
\right)\varepsilon_{t-i-1}.
\]

Therefore,
\[
\mathrm{Var}\left(\hat y_t\right)
=
\left(
V_{yz}^2
+
\sum_{i=0}^{\infty}
\left[
V_{yk}V_{kz}\sum_{j=0}^{i}V_{kk}^{\,j}\psi^{\,i-j}
+
V_{yz}\psi^{i+1}
\right]^2
\right)\mathrm{Var}\left(\varepsilon_t\right).
\]

Given parameters' value, we can back out the explicit numerical solution.

This model works:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\arabic*)]
\item well for consumption volatility
\item bad for output volatility: requires too small TFP volatility
\end{enumerate}

\begin{enumerate}[label=\arabic*), start=3]
\item bad for hours volatility $\Rightarrow$ way too low.
\item bad for investment volatility $\Rightarrow$ far too low.
\end{enumerate}

But it allows $N_t$ to move with shocks compared to Long--Plosser. While with this set of preference and technology, it cannot generate enough labor and investment volatility relative to output.

\section{Hansen (1985) with indivisible labor}

Labor market phenomena:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\arabic*)]
\item Hours fluctuate a lot relative to productivity.
\item Micro evidence does not support huge intertemporal substitution of labor at individual level.
\end{enumerate}

Target: get big aggregate hours fluctuations without assuming each person is very willing to shift leisure across time.

Key mechanism:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\arabic*)]
\item Each individual's labor is a corner-choice:
\[
\tilde n_t \in \left\{0,n^*\right\}.
\]
Either work a fixed shift or no work.
\item Lottery over jobs: assigned a job with probability $\pi_t$.
\item Complete unemployment insurance: $C_t$ is independent of job status.
\end{enumerate}

\textcolor{red}{Crucial: we can write expected utility clearly at the ``representative agent'' level.}

Thus total labor supply:
\[
H_t=\bar L\,\pi_t n^*
\]
where $\bar L$ is total labor force.

Average labor supply:
\[
n_t=\frac{H_t}{\bar L}=\pi_t n^*.
\]

Representative-agent utility: individual utility is
\[
U\left(C_t\right)+v\left(\tilde n_t\right).
\]

Given lottery,
\[
E\left[U\left(C_t\right)+v\left(\tilde n_t\right)\right]
=
U\left(C_t\right)+\pi_t v\left(n^*\right)+\left(1-\pi_t\right)v\left(0\right).
\]

Let
\[
v\left(0\right)=0,
\qquad
\frac{v\left(n^*\right)}{n^*}=-A<0.
\]
Then
\[
E\left[U\left(C_t\right)+v\left(\tilde n_t\right)\right]
=
U\left(C_t\right)+\pi_t\left(-An^*\right)
=
U\left(C_t\right)-An_t.
\]

Suppose
\[
U\left(C_t\right)=\ln C_t
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
U_t=\ln C_t - A n_t.
\]

Hence
\[
U_{C,t}=\frac{1}{C_t},
\qquad
U_{N,t}=-A,
\qquad
-U_{N,t}=U_{C,t}W_t
\]

Micro-level: no smoothing in labor, either $n^*$ or $0$, keep small intertemporal labor substitution.

Macro-level: planner / representative agent can vary $\pi_t$ a lot, aggregate hours become very responsive.

Thus, we can define the equilibrium conditions for this model:
\[
\begin{cases}
1=\beta E_t\left[\dfrac{C_t}{C_{t+1}}\left(\alpha Z_{t+1}K_t^{\alpha-1}N_{t+1}^{1-\alpha}+1-\delta\right)\right],\\[1em]
K_t=Z_tK_{t-1}^{\alpha}N_t^{1-\alpha}+\left(1-\delta\right)K_{t-1}-C_t,\\[0.8em]
AC_t=\left(1-\alpha\right)Z_tK_{t-1}^{\alpha}N_t^{-\alpha},
\qquad
\textcolor{red}{\text{key difference}}
\qquad
-U_{N,t}=U_{C,t}W_t=U_{C,t}MPL_t,\\[0.8em]
\log Z_t=\left(1-\psi\right)\log \bar Z+\psi\log Z_{t-1}+\varepsilon_t.
\end{cases}
\]

\paragraph{Mechanism}\mbox{}\\
In the indivisible labor model, aggregate hours fluctuate mainly through the employment margin:
more people work rather than each individual continuously adjusting hours.

By contrast,in the divisible labor model,
\[
TFP\uparrow
\Rightarrow
\text{demand for labor}\uparrow
\Rightarrow
W_t\uparrow
\Rightarrow
N_t\uparrow
\Rightarrow
1-N_t\downarrow
\]

\[
\ln\left(1-N_t\right)
\text{ becomes steeper}
\Rightarrow
\text{the cost of giving up a marginal hour of leisure rises}
\]

\[
\Rightarrow\quad
\text{unwilling to adjust }N_t,\ \text{wage moves more} \Rightarrow \text{stiff labor}
\]
\[
\Rightarrow\quad
\text{small hour volatility.}
\qquad
\text{(each person chooses hours continuously)}
\]
\paragraph{Consistency with facts}

\[
n_t=\frac{H_t}{\bar L}=\frac{H_t}{L_t}\cdot \frac{L_t}{\bar L}
\approx n^*\left(1-u_t\right)=n^*\pi_t,
\]
where $u_t$ is the unemployment rate.

\[
\frac{H_t}{L_t}=\bar n_t \approx n^*
\qquad
\text{is empirically stable.}
\]

*Cyclical movement in total hours comes from (extensive margin) employment, not from hours per worker (intensive margin).

\section{Other RBC models}

\begin{enumerate}[label=\arabic*.]
\item CRRA utility (King and Rebelo, 1999):
\[
U\left(C_t,L_t\right)=\ln C_t + A\frac{\left(1-N_t\right)^{1-\chi}-1}{1-\chi}.
\]

Investment volatility $\uparrow\uparrow$ (almost correct), but underpredicts persistence, over-predicts comovement; consumption \& labor are less volatile in model than data.

\textcolor{red}{change preference curvature and more volatile TFP}

\item KPR utility (King, Plosser and Rebelo, 1988):
\[
U\left(C_t,L_t\right)=\frac{\left[C_t\,v\left(L_t\right)\right]^{1-\sigma}-1}{1-\sigma}.
\]

Non-separable preference between $C$ and $N$.
$U_{C,t}$ depends on $L_t$, $U_{L_t}$ depends on $C_t$.
Relax the too-tight linkages $\Rightarrow$ improve comovement patterns.

\item GHH utility (Greenwood--Hercowitz--Huffman, 1988, AER)
\[
U\left(C_t,N_t\right)
=
\frac{1}{1-\sigma}
\left[
\left(
C_t - A\frac{N_t^{1+\psi}}{1+\psi}
\right)^{1-\sigma}
-1
\right].
\]

Cancel wealth effect, labor supply responds to substitution effect (wage/productivity), $\Rightarrow$ even more volatile $N_t$.

Since
\[
-U_{N,t}=U_{C,t}W_t
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
AN_t^\psi=W_t.
\]

\item Capital utilization:
\[
Y_t = Z_t \left(u_t K_{t-1}\right)^{\alpha}N_t^{1-\alpha},
\]
\[
K_t = I_t + \left[1-\delta\left(u_t\right)\right]K_{t-1},
\qquad
\delta\left(u\right)>0
\]

Extra intensive margin for capital $\Rightarrow$ higher investment volatility, higher output volatility.
\end{enumerate}
